Description:

Durga Puja also known as Durgotsava, or Sharodotsava, is an annual Hindu festival originating in the Indian subcontinent which reveres and pays homage to the Hindu goddess Durga. It is particularly popular and traditionally celebrated in the Indian states of West Bengal, Bihar, Assam, Odisha, Tripura and the country of Bangaldesh, and the diaspora from this region, and also in Mithianchal regions of Bihar and Nepal. The festival is observed in the Indian calendar month of Ashwin, which corresponds to the months of September-October in the Georgian calendar, and is a ten day festival, of which the last five are of significance. The puja is performed at homes and publicly, the latter featuring temporary stage and structural decoration (known as pandals). Durga puja is an important festival in the Shaktism Tradition of Hinduism.

As per Hindu scriptures, the festival marks the victory of goddess Durga in her battle againt the shape-shifting asura or Mahishasura. Thus, the festival epitomises the victory of good over evil.

The primary goddess revered during Durga puja is Durga but celebrations also include other major deities of Hinduism such as Lakshmi (the goddess of wealth and prosperity), Saraswati (the goddess of Knowledge and music), Ganesh (the god of good beginnings and wealth), and Kartikeya (the god of war). In Bengali tradition, these deities are considered to be Durga’s children and Durga puja is believed to commemorate Durga’s visit to her natal home with her beloved and nice children. The festival is preceded by Mahalaya, which is belived to mark the start of Durga’s journey to her natal home.Primary celebration begin on the sixth day (Sasthi), on which the goddess is welcomed with rituals. The festival ends on the tenth day (Vijaya dashami), when the devotees embark on a procession carrying the worshipped clay sculpture-idols to a river, or other water body, and immerse them, symbolic of her return to the divine cosmos and her martial home with Shiva">Shiva in Kailash. Regional and community variations in celebration of the festival and rituals observed exist.
