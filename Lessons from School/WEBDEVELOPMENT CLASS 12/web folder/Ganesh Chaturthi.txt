<font face="STXingkai" size="6" color="red"><b><u><i>About Ganesh Chaturthi</i></b></u></font>

<p ALIGN="left"><font color="black"size="5" face="Stliti"><b><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganesh_Chaturthi"><font face="Forte" size="5" color="blue"><b>Ganesh Chaturthi</b></font></a> is a Hindu festival which is also known as <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganesh_Chaturthi"><font face="Forte" size="5" color="blue">Vinayaka Chaturthi</font></a>.<br>
It is celebrated as the arival of Ganesh from Kailash Parvat to Earth. This festival is marked with the installation of Ganesh clay idols privately in homes and publicly  in elaborate pandals in temporary stages. 
Observations include chanting of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vedas"><font face="Forte" size="5" color="blue">Vedic hymns and Hindu texts </font></a> such as, <font face="Forte" size="5" color="green">prayers and vrata</font> offerings 
and prasāda from the daily prayers, that are distributed from the pandal to the community, include sweets such as
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modak"><font face="Forte" size="5" color="blue">modaka</font></a> as it believed to be a favourite of Lord Ganesh.
</b></font>

<body>
<p><font color="black"size="5" face="Stliti"><b><img src="https://images.hindustantimes.com/rf/image_size_960x540/HT/p2/2020/08/22/Pictures/eve-of-ganesh-chaturthi-festival_f356ac0a-e438-11ea-aae4-2b178f7f5029.jpg" height="150" width="250"align="right"/>  The festival ends on the tenth day after start, when the idol is carried in a public procession with music and group
 chanting, then immersed in a nearby body of water such as a river or sea.
In <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mumbai"><font face="forte" size="5" color="blue">Mumbai</font></a> alone, around <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">150,000</font> statues are immersed annually. Thereafter the clay idol dissolves and
 Ganesh is believed to return to Mount Kailash to Parvati and Shiva. The festival celebrates <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganesha"><font face="forte" size="5" color="blue">Lord Ganesh</font></a> as the <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">God of New
 Beginnings</font> and the <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">Remover of Obstacles</font> as well as the <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">god of wisdom and intelligence</font>  and is
 observed throughout <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India"><font face="forte" size="5" color="blue">India</font></a>, especially
 in the states such as <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">Maharashtra, Goa, Karnataka, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Odisha, West Bengal, Gujarat, Chhatishgarh and Tamil Nadu</font>. <br> 
 <font face="forte" size="5" color="red">Ganesh Chaturthi</font> is also observed in <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">Nepal</font> and by <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">the Hindu diaspora</font> 
 elsewhere such as in <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">Australia, New Zealand, Canada, Singapore, Suriname</font>, other parts of the <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">Caribbean, Fiji,Mauritius, South Africa, United States, and Europe</font>.
  In the <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">Gregorian calendar</font>, <font face="forte" size="5" color="red">Ganesh Chaturthi</font> falls between <font face="forte" size="5" color="green">22 August and 20 September</font> every year.</p>
