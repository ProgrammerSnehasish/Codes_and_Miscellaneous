<p><u><font color="red" size="6" face="STXingkai">Basic details:<font></u></p><p><img src="https://www.nkrealtors.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/idol-painting-at-kumortuli-730x410.jpg" height="140" width="260" align="left"/><font color="black" size="5" face="STLiti"><b><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Durga_Puja"><font color="blue"><u>Durga Puja</u></font></a> is the most important festival in the city of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kolkata"><font color="blue"><u>Kolkata</u></font></a>. The festival celebrates the victory of good over evil. This festival is celebrated for five days and marked by prayers to goddess Durga, feasts, rejoicing, music, dance and drama. On this day, the people wear new clothes and visit all the community Pujas and worship of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Durga"><font color="blue"><u>ten-armed</u></font></a>.<img src="https://www.orangewayfarer.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/11/DSC_0257-01.jpg" height="140" width="260" align="right"/> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Durga"><font color="blue"><u>Goddess Durga</u></font></a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saraswati"><font color="blue"><u>Saraswati</u></font></a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lakshmi"><font color="blue"><u>Lakshmi</u></font></a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganesha"><font color="blue"><u>Ganesh</u></font></a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kartikeya"><font color="blue"><u>Kartikeya</u></font></a> in beautifully illuminated and decorated <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pandal"><font color="blue"><u>pandols</u></font></a>. The priests perform the prayers at appointed times while the devotees visit these pandals throughout the day. In towns and villages, in the evening, drama, song, music, dance programmes, sports, physical and cultural competitions etc are held in which everyone participates. Community feasts are also held.</b></font></p>

<p><u><font color="red" size="6" face="STXingkai">Description:<font></u></p><p><font color="black" size="5" face="STLiti"><b><font color="blue">Durga Puja</font> aslo known as <font color="blue">Durgotsava</font>, or <font color="blue">Sharodotsava</font>, is an annual Hindu festival originating in the Indian subcontinent which reveres and pays homage to the Hindu goddess Durga. It is particularly popular and traditionally celebrated in the Indian states of <font color="blue">West Bengal</font>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bihar"><font color="blue"><u>Bihar</u></font></a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assam"><font color="blue"><u>Assam</u></font></a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odisha"><font color="blue"><u>Odisha</u></font></a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tripura"><font color="blue"><u>Tripura</u></font></a> and the country of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh"><font color="blue"><u>Bangaldesh</u></font></a>, and the diaspora from this region, and also in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mithila_(region)"><font color="blue"><u>Mithianchal</u></font></a> regions of Bihar and Nepal. The festival is observed in the Indian calendar month of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashvin_(month)"><font color="blue"><u>Ashwin</u></font></a>, which corresponds to the months of <font color="blue">September-October</font> in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregorian_calendar"><font color="blue"><u>Georgian calendar</u></font></a>, and is a ten day festival, of which the last five are of significance. The puja is performed at homes and publicly, the latter featuring temporary stage and structural decoration (known as <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pandal"><font color="blue"><u>pandals</u></font></a>). Durga puja is an important festival in the Shaktism Tradition of Hinduism. </b></font></p>

<p><font color="black" size="5" face="STLiti"><b>As per <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vedas"><font color="blue"><u>Hindu scriptures</u></font></a>, the festival marks the victory of goddess Durga in her battle againt the shape-shifting <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahishasura"><font color="blue"><u>asura or Mahishasura</u></font></a>. Thus, the festival epitomises the victory of good over evil.</b></font></p>

<p><img src="https://thefederal.com/file/2019/07/Durga-puja-PTI.jpg" height="140" width="260" align="left"/><font color="black" size="5" face="STLiti"><b>The primary goddess revered during Durga puja is <font color="blue">Durga</font> but celebrations also include other major deities of Hinduism such as <font color="blue">Lakshmi</font> (the goddess of wealth and prosperity), <font color="blue">Saraswati</font> (the goddess of Knowledge and music), <font color="blue">Ganesh</font> (the god of good beginnings and wealth), and <font color="blue">Kartikeya</font> (the god of war). In Bengali tradition, these deities are considered to be Durga’s children and Durga puja is believed to commemorate Durga’s visit to her natal home with her beloved and nice children.<img src="https://edge-cdn.pratidintime.com/2019/10/screenshot2019-10-08-08_30_12-1-650x430.jpg" height="140" width="260" align="right"/> The festival is preceded by <font color="blue">Mahalaya</font>, which is belived to mark the start of Durga’s journey to her natal home.Primary celebration begin on the sixth day (<font color="blue">Sasthi</font>), on which the goddess is welcomed with rituals. The festival ends on the tenth day (<font color="blue">Vijaya dashami</font>), when the devotees embark on a procession carrying the worshipped clay sculpture-idols to a river, or other water body, and immerse them, symbolic of her return to the divine cosmos and her martial home with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shiva"><font color="blue"><u>Shiva</u></font></a> in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Kailash"><font color="blue"><u>Kailash</u></font></a>. Regional and community variations in celebration of the festival and rituals observed exist. </b></font></p>
